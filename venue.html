The Course

Hudson Valley's Finest

Established in 1931 and expanded to 18 holes in the mid-1990s, Red Hook Golf Club has grown from a modest local nine into one of Dutchess County's premier semi-private destinations. The course has hosted the New York State Men's Amateur Championship, the Dutchess County Men's Amateur, and the Dutchess County Senior Amateur Championships.

The course plays to a par of 72 and stretches 6,564 yards from the back tees. The front nine winds through open, modern design with risk-reward opportunities — most notably the par-5 3rd hole where a lake sits dead center in the fairway. The back nine narrows into tighter, tree-lined parkland that rewards accuracy over power.

More Than Golf

The Grove at Red Hook

Our on-site restaurant, The Grove, handles the lunch service. The on-site restaurant serves fresh, local, high-quality food — everything from grilled local vegetables to smoked meats. With shaded tent seating, live music, and misters and heaters for October mild, lunch is the centerpiece of the day.
